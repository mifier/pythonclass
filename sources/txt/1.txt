Aim  this study aims to explore how they  understand and (re)construct their academic identity from nurse academics’ perspectives. 
Method this study used a purposive sampling strategy.  fifteen participants with a range of seniority and experience as both academics and nurses were recruited from the nursing school in two local New Zealand universities. This study using one-on-one semi-structured interviews. The interview schedule included open-ended questions regarding the participant’s experiences of career development, the defining characteristics of their nursing and academic identities and the tensions or difficulties of the identity transition/development.
